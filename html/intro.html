Introduction to HTML

What is XHTML?

XHTML stands for eXtensible HyperText Markup Language and is aimed to replace HTML. XHTML is almost identical to HTML 4.01 and a stricter and cleaner version of HTML.

Markup language means a method of segmenting the content by marking the beginning and ending of the segment. This is the example:

HTML 
<b>this part is bold</b>
and this part is not

Hypertext means that the marked segment can be linked to another document, another object or another part of the current document.

Extensible means that the whole language can be expanded, new markups can be created by using combinations of standard ones.
